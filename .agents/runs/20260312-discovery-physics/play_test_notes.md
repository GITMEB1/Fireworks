## Play-test: Basic Click (Test A)
- Feeling: neutral
- What worked: High responsiveness, instant launch.
- What felt missing: Visual variety, spread.
- Friction points: Repeated clicks feel identical.
- Delight spikes: None.

## Play-test: Short Hold (Test B)
- Feeling: delightful
- What worked: Cyan/green charge ring feedback, larger/denser explosions.
- What felt missing: A way to "bank" or "combo" these.
- Friction points: None.
- Delight spikes: Seeing the ring fill up and the subsequent burst.

## Play-test: Perfect Charge (Test C)
- Feeling: frustrating
- What worked: The visual payoff (when hit).
- What felt missing: Forgiveness window.
- Friction points: Extremely narrow success window (95-99%).
- Delight spikes: Impactful supernova (when successful).

## Play-test: Overcharge (Test D)
- Feeling: frustrating
- What worked: N/A
- What felt missing: Visual warning (overheat), alternative "dirty" explosion.
- Friction points: Sudden failure/fizzle with no payoff.
- Delight spikes: None.

## Play-test: Sustained Rapid Fire (Test E)
- Feeling: neutral/cluttered
- What worked: High particle performance.
- What felt missing: Reward for high APM (Actions Per Minute).
- Friction points: Screen clutter, visual fatigue.
- Delight spikes: Initial intensity.

## Retention/Engagement Drop-off
The drop-off occurs around **60 seconds** once the player realizes that pushing for a high charge is more likely to result in a "fizzle" than a "supernova", and there are no higher-tier goals or variety to keep them engaged.

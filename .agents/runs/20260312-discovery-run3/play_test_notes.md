## Play-test: Test A - Basic click
- Feeling: delightful
- What worked: Immediate, snappy response. Good color variety.
- What felt missing: Heavy impact on a basic click; it's very light.
- Friction points: None, extremely responsive.
- Delight spikes: The instant burst of randomized particles.

## Play-test: Test B - Short hold (20-40%)
- Feeling: delightful
- What worked: Clear charge indicator ring, noticeable increase in explosion size and density.
- What felt missing: The rocket ascent could change strictly based on this charge.
- Friction points: None.
- Delight spikes: Seeing the considerably larger, denser explosion after just a short wait.

## Play-test: Test C - Perfect charge (95-99%)
- Feeling: delightful
- What worked: Time dilation, violent screen shake, and brilliant white-blue flash combine for an incredible high-impact moment.
- What felt missing: Hard to improve on this, it's very juicy.
- Friction points: Timing it perfectly can be slightly tricky, but that's part of the fun.
- Delight spikes: The screen shake and time dilation effect hitting simultaneously.

## Play-test: Test D - Overcharge (Fizzle)
- Feeling: frustrating (by design)
- What worked: Clear visual penalty (dull grey ring vibrates and vanishes, small grey smoke puff).
- What felt missing: An audio cue would sell the "dud" feeling even more.
- Friction points: Losing the accumulated charge.
- Delight spikes: The tension of trying to avoid it while pushing the limit.

## Play-test: Test E - Sustained rapid fire
- Feeling: neutral
- What worked: Perfect performance and stability under heavy load.
- What felt missing: A combo multiplier or escalating reward for rapid, sustained clicking.
- Friction points: High visual fatigue due to overlapping bright flashes over time.
- Delight spikes: The sheer volume of particles and overlapping glow effects.

## Drop-off moment:
The drop-off moment occurs after the player has experienced the "Perfect Charge" supernova a few times. Once they realize there are only two primary interactions (rapid click or charge-to-supernova) and no meta-progression, chaining combo system, or changing scenarios, the physical novelty wears off (typically around the 2-3 minute mark). There is no incentive to keep "playing".
